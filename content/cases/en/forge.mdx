## The origin

I built the first version for myself. No signup. No database. Everything hardcoded for one person.

It had an AI coach connected to my real schedule — it didn't just answer questions, it took actions: rescheduled tasks, tracked habits, and read my diary entries to respond like a psychologist who actually knew my week. XP, streaks, achievements. I used it every day.

Then I showed it to friends. They wanted it too.

---

## How it went: 4 steps

**① The personal version worked too well**

The AI knew everything: my mood from journal entries, my task backlog, my schedule gaps. It acted on that context — not "here's advice" but "I've updated your Tuesday, here's why."

That's when I realised the idea had legs beyond just me.

**② The hard part — making personal code work for everyone**

My name, my goals, my tracks were baked into the code. Every user needed their own space: their own AI context, their own diary, their own schedule. Nothing could be hardcoded anymore.

The challenge wasn't just technical. It was keeping the feeling of the original — that the AI actually *knows* you — while making it work for a stranger on day one.

**③ What got rebuilt**

Three things had to change completely:

- **Auth** — users sign up, log in, own their data. Supabase handled identity and row-level security so no one sees anyone else's diary
- **Database** — journal entries, habits, streaks, schedule — all moved from localStorage to Supabase. Data that persists, syncs, and survives a browser clear
- **Email** — the AI doesn't only live in the chat window. Weekly summaries, streak reminders, and achievement unlocks go out automatically

**④ Launch — two beta rounds, real bugs, real fixes**

First beta found auth issues. Broke for some users on login. Fixed. Second round — smoother. Edge cases in the achievement system. Fixed.

After the second round: stable enough to open up. Custom domain, live environment, real users.

## What Forge does

**AI diary with memory** — write about your day, the AI responds as a coach. It reads previous entries and builds a picture of your patterns over time. Not generic advice — responses shaped by what you've actually written.

**Schedule in plain language** — describe your week, the AI organises it, flags what's urgent, and suggests where you're overloading yourself.

**Habits + gamification** — streaks, XP, achievement badges. The kind of system that makes you want to open the app tomorrow just to keep the streak alive.

**Psychology mode** — the AI uses your journal context to ask the right questions at the right time. It notices when you've mentioned something three weeks in a row.
